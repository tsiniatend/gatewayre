CULTURAL COFFEE

The beautiful and unique coffee ceremony is a tradition in Ethiopia which takes time to celebrate friendship, hospitality, and respect. It begins by spreading fresh flowers on the ground, while burning incense to make the air more fragrant. The coffee beans are then brewed in a traditional mortar before putting them in a "Jebena." Don't be in a hurry though - this special ceremony can take a few hours. While it isn't as fast or modern as Starbucks, the coffee is just the centerpiece of an elegant ceremony meant to be shared with family and guests.

VEGAN VARIETY

Ethiopian food is one of the world’s most flavourful cuisines. It is also extremely welcoming to vegans because of the countries blending of many herbs and grains into meals. Though just like their Coffee Cermonies, Ethiopians have a strong sense of community which is displayed in their communal style of eating. This is usually present in the form of thick, rich, spicy wat (stews), seasoned sauteed vegetables, sauces, and salads served on large pieces of injera (sourdough flatbread) for multiple people to eat.

WILD WONDERLUST

Ethiopia has an astonishing range of wildlife; boasting over 22,000 species of butterflies and moths, over 800 bird species, and about 320 species of mammals. The highlands are the source of four major river systems that spread across Africa, all depicting the bueaty of nature.The capital city Addis Ababa is located in the Western highlands and is Africa’s highest capital city known as "The Horn of Africa!. It is also known to be the last remaining home of many animals including the ONLY place to find the BLACK LION!
